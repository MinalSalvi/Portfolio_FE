Violence Against Women:

Violence against women is a severe issue in India, with incidents of rape, domestic violence, and sexual harassment being widespread. Gender inequality exacerbates this issue, as women are often viewed as subservient to men and are not given equal protection under the law. This lack of protection perpetuates a culture of impunity for perpetrators of gender-based violence, making it challenging for victims to seek justice. Addressing gender inequality is critical to reducing violence against women, ensuring that women are protected and have equal access to justice.

Empowering Women:

Gender equality helps reduce violence against women, including domestic violence, sexual harassment, and rape. It also ensures that women have equal protection under the law and can seek justice if they are victims of such crimes.

Boosting the Economy

Gender equality can have a positive impact on the economy. When women are empowered, they can contribute to economic growth by participating in the workforce and starting their own businesses.
